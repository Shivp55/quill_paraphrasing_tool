Question

Your company, Q4 Inc., is considering a new project whose data are shown below. The required equipment has a 3-year tax life, and the MACRS rates for such property are 33%, 45%, 15%, and 7% for Years 1 through 4. Revenues and other operating costs are expected to be constant over the project's 10-year operating life. What is the project's operating cash flow during Year 4?

Equipment cost (depreciable basis) $70,000
Sales revenues, each year $50,000
Operating costs excl. depr'n $25,000
Tax rate 35.0%

a. $16,213
b. $17,067
c. $17,965
d. $18,863
e. $19,806


Answer

Total answers posted by the expert is: 11246

The operating cash flow during year 4 is computed as shown below:

= Earnings before interest and tax + depreciation - tax expenses

Earnings before interest and tax is computed as follows:

= Sales - operating cost excluding depreciation - depreciation

Depreciation is computed as follows:

= Equipment cost x depreciation rate 

= $ 70,000 x 7%

= $ 4,900

So, the EBIT will be as follows:

= $ 50,000 - $ 25,000 - $ 4,900

= $ 20,100

Tax expenses is computed as follows:

= EBIT x tax rate

= $ 20,100 x 35%

= $ 7,035

So, the operating cash flow will be as follows:

= $ 20,100 + $ 4,900 - $ 7,035

= $ 17,965

So, the correct answer is option c.

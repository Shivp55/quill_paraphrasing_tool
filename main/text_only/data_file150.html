Question

Assuming that the isothermal coefficient of compressibility is constant, determine the percent decrease in the volume of liquid water that undergoes an isothermal increase in pressure of 100 x 10^3 psia


Answer

Total answers posted by the expert is: 6631

isothermal coefficient of compressibility of water = 4.6*10^-10 Pa^-1 = K
Change in pressure = delta P = 100*10^3 psi = 689475729 Pa
Thus deltaV/V = delta P * K = 0.3172
=>Percent change = 31.72 %

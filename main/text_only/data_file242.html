Question

Three charges are arranged in the (x,y) plane as shown.

(0,7)= 5nC, (0,0)= 9nC, and (7,0)=3nC

What angle does the resultant force on the 9nc charge at the origin make with the positive x axis? Quadrant I lies between 0 degrees and 90 degrees, quadrant II between 90 and 180 degrees, etc. Answer in units of degrees.

Please advise on how to solve

Force on the charge qo due to q1 is
Fx = kqoq1/x ^2 toward left (because of repulsion)

Force on the charge qo due to q2 is
Fy = kqoq2/y ^2 toward down (because of attrection).

Resultant of these two is
v (Fx^2 + Fy^2)
= kqo v {q1/x^2 + q2/y^2}

q1/x^2 = 3.83e-9/ 0.628^2 = 9.7e-9

q2/y^2 = - 2.49 e-9/0.147^2 =1.15e-7

kqo = 8.98755e ^9*5.99e-9 = 8.e-6

kqo v {q1/x^2 + q2/y^2} = 2.825e-9

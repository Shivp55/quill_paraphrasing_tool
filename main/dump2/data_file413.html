Solve the following initial value problem: t*dy/dt + 6 y = 8t with y(1) = 2 I am having trouble with finding the integration factor and also when it comes to doing the reverse product rule. I can't seem to connect the dots.

( dy(t))/( dt)+(6 y(t))/t = 8
Let mu(t) = exp( integral 6/t dt) = t^6.
Multiply both sides by mu(t):
t^6 ( dy(t))/( dt)+(6 t^5) y(t) = 8 t^6
Substitute 6 t^5 = ( d)/( dt)(t^6):
t^6 ( dy(t))/( dt)+( d)/( dt)(t^6) y(t) = 8 t^6
Apply the reverse product rule f ( dg)/( dt)+( df)/( dt) g = ( d)/( dt)(f g) to the left-hand side:
( d)/( dt)(t^6 y(t)) = 8 t^6
Integrate both sides with respect to t:
integral ( d)/( dt)(t^6 y(t)) dt = integral 8 t^6 dt
Evaluate the integrals:

Divide both sides by mu(t) = t^6:

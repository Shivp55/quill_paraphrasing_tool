Question

Urn A: 2 white, 4 red
Urn B: 8 white, 4 red
Urn C: 1 white, 3 red
Suppose we select a ball from A (randomly) and transfer it to B, mix the balls in B,
select one and transfer it to C, then draw one ball from Urn C. What is the probability that it is red?


Answer

Urn A has 2 white and 4 red.
Therefore, the probability that a red is selected is 2/3, and the probability that a white is selected is 1/3.
Then, the probability that urn b has 8 whites and 5 reds = 2/3, and the probability that urn b has 9 whites and 4 reds = 1/3
Then, the probability that urn c adds a red and is 1 white and 4 reds is
2/3 * 5/13 (5 of 13 are red) + 1/3 * 4/13 = 14/39
The probability that urn c adds a white and is 2 whites and 3 reds is
2/3 * 8/13 (8 of 13 are white) + 1/3 * 9/13 = 25/39

Then, the probability that a red is drawn from C =
14/39 * 4/5 (4 of 5 are red)+ 25/39 * 3/5 = 131/195

Question

The kidneys function to eliminate ammonia from the blood. Based on the pK value, what is the molecular form that predominates in the blood? Could this molecule easily diffuse through th ehydrophobic lipid membrane of a kidney cell? Explain.


Answer

Total answers posted by the expert is: 534

The blood has a pH of 7.4. Because the pKa value of ammonia (NH3) is 9.3, it will tend to extract a hydrogen from the blood and exist as ammonium cation (NH4+). Because this is a charged molecule, it is unlikely to diffuse (passive transport) through hydrophobic lipid membranes. Instead it requires the use of channels or membrane transport proteins to pass ammonium across the membrane.
